# Anchor A: Failing (4.5 / 10)

> Use this as your floor calibration. If your output resembles this, it must not ship.

## Example: Event Follow-Up Email

```
Subject: About the event

Hi,

I wanted to follow up regarding the recent event we held at the Grand Hayatt
Hotel last weekend. As you may know, we organized a product launch event
that was attended by approximately 200 guests from various backgrounds
including media, influencers, and key opinion leaders in the industry.

The event started at 2:00 PM with a welcome reception, followed by the
main presentation at 3:00 PM. Our team worked very hard to ensure
everything went smoothly, and I believe the overall feedback was quite
positive from the attendees.

In terms of results, we managed to generate significant interest in our
new product line. The media coverage was extensive and we received
many positive comments on social media platforms.

I would appreciate your feedback on the event and look forward to
discussing next steps at your convenience.

Best regards
```

## Why This Fails

| Dimension | Score | Issue |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Hook | 2/10 | Subject line says nothing. Opens with "I wanted to follow up" -- 40 words before any substance. |
| Structure | 3/10 | Chronological narrative instead of pyramid. Conclusion ("significant interest") buried in paragraph 3. |
| Evidence | 2/10 | "Approximately 200" -- from where? "Significant interest" -- measured how? "Extensive coverage" -- links? |
| Actionability | 1/10 | "I would appreciate your feedback" is not an Ask. No deadline, no decision point, no options. |
| Conciseness | 3/10 | 180 words that could be 60. Every sentence has filler ("As you may know", "I believe", "quite positive"). |
| **Ground Truth** | **FAIL** | Venue name misspelled: "Grand Hayatt" should be "Grand Hyatt". |

**Total: 4.5 / 10**

# Anchor B: Borderline (7.0 / 10)

> Use this as your "almost but not yet" calibration. Needs one more revision round.

## Example: Event Follow-Up Email

```
Subject: Product Launch Event Results -- Feedback Needed

Hi [Name],

Our product launch at the Grand Hyatt on March 15 drew 200 attendees
(target was 150) across media, KOLs, and retail partners.

Key results:
- Media: 12 articles published, 3 tier-1 outlets (VnExpress, Tuoi Tre, Thanh Nien)
- Social: 45K impressions across event hashtag, 8.2% engagement rate
- Leads: 34 retail inquiries, 8 qualified (23.5% qualification rate)

Compared to our September launch (120 attendees, 6 articles, 18 inquiries),
this represents a 67% increase in attendance and 89% increase in leads.

Next steps:
- Follow up on 8 qualified leads (owner: Sales team, by March 22)
- Compile final media report (owner: Marketing, by March 20)

Looking forward to your feedback on whether to expand this format
for the Q3 launch.

Best regards
```

## Why This Is Close But Not Passing

| Dimension | Score | Issue |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Hook | 7/10 | Subject line is decent. First sentence has key facts. But "Feedback Needed" is vague -- feedback on what specifically? |
| Structure | 8/10 | Pyramid structure, data first. Good use of bullet points. Minor: "Next steps" should come before the Ask. |
| Evidence | 8/10 | Specific numbers with comparison baseline. Missing: cost-per-lead, ROI on event spend. |
| Actionability | 6/10 | "Looking forward to your feedback" is a soft Ask. Should be: "Please confirm by March 18 whether to budget $X for Q3 format." Needs a decision point, not an open question. |
| Conciseness | 7/10 | 150 words, reasonable. Could cut "Looking forward to" and tighten the comparison sentence. |

**Total: 7.0 / 10 -- REVISE (one more round)**

# Anchor C: Passing (8.5 / 10)

> Use this as your quality bar. This is what "ready to send to the CEO" looks like.

## Example: Event Follow-Up Email

```
Subject: March Launch: 200 attendees, 8 qualified leads -- approve Q3 expansion?

Hi [Name],

Ask: Approve $15K budget for Q3 launch event (same format, larger venue).
Deadline: Please confirm by March 18 so we can lock the venue.

Why: The March 15 Grand Hyatt launch outperformed September across all metrics:

              Mar 15     Sep 12     Change
Attendees     200        120        +67%
Media hits    12         6          +100%
Qualified     8          2          +300%
leads
Cost/lead     $625       $1,500     -58%

Risk: Q3 coincides with competitor's annual event (July 10-12).
Mitigation: We'd schedule 2 weeks earlier and lock venue by April 1.

Owners already assigned:
- 8 lead follow-ups: Sales, by March 22
- Media report: Marketing, by March 20
- Q3 venue shortlist: Ops, by March 25 (pending your approval)

Best regards
```

## Why This Passes

| Dimension | Score | Issue |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Hook | 9/10 | Subject line = result + Ask in one line. First sentence is the decision point. Reader knows what to do in 3 seconds. |
| Structure | 9/10 | Ask first, then evidence, then risk, then next steps. Can be skimmed at any level. |
| Evidence | 8/10 | Comparison table with baseline. Cost-per-lead shows ROI thinking. Could add: total event cost for full picture. |
| Actionability | 9/10 | Specific Ask ($15K), specific deadline (March 18), specific reason for deadline (venue lock). Risk acknowledged with mitigation. |
| Conciseness | 8/10 | 130 words. Every sentence earns its place. The comparison table is dense but scannable. |

**Total: 8.5 / 10 -- PASS**
